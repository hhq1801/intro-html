Henry III Fine Rolls project

Project URL

http://www.frh3.org.uk 

Summary

The Henry III Fine Rolls Project offers a window into English history 1216 - 1272.

The project democratises access to the fine rolls - records of money offered to Henry III for a multiplicity of concessions and favours - by making them freely available in English translation on the web. The edition comprises a full translation of the rolls in electronic form with indexes and a search facility; digital facsimile images; 'Fine of the Month' essays featuring material of interest in the rolls; and corresponding print volumes of translations with full indexes published by Boydell and Brewer. An additional output will be a book analysing the history of Henry's reign through the evidence to be found in the Fine Rolls.

The project uses technologies traditionally associated with knowledge management and ontological representation to express complex associations between entities in historical texts that have been marked up in XML according to the Text Encoding Initiative guidelines. In particular, research is focussing on the potential use of Semantic Web technologies and the role of a formal ontology in facilitating the interpretation of implicit and hidden associations in historical sources.
