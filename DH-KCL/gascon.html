The Gascon Rolls Project (1317-1468)

Project URL

http://www.gasconrolls.org/ 

Summary

The Gascon Rolls Project created a searchable digital resource giving unique access to a series of documents which constitute the major source for Plantagenet rule in Aquitaine.

The Gascon Rolls (a series of records of the English chancery covering the duchy of medieval Aquitaine) are of fundamental importance, shedding considerable light on English government in the last major continental possession of the English Crown, and revealing the relationship between the king, and his English administration, with his officers in the duchy, and with his subjects in his lordship of Aquitaine.

The project developed a methodology which allows historians to produce a full calendar (summary translation) edition of the rolls with extensive indexes and a search tool which permits highly sophisticated querying on people, places and subjects, in addition to high quality digital images of the original rolls. The digital framework used to model, edit and publish the rolls drew on research carried out on other historical projects at DDH (and beyond) which use semantic web technology as a basis for representing scholarly assertions about historical sources. The framework created has been the basis for further funding of the project, which includes a significant element of digital humanities research:

  • Rôles Gascons en ligne / Online Gascon Rolls
  • Old wine in new bottles: English Gascony (1360-1453) for the Digital Future
