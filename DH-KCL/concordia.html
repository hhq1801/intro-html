Summary

The overall aim of the project was to make it easier for readers to move between publications on the Web, instead of walking from one library shelf to another. Bringing information together in this way helps researchers to recognise a new range of relationships and interactions.
This project brings together the several epigraphic resources created at the CCH in London (two volumes of inscriptions from Aphrodisias, and two of material from Libya) with the Pleiades ancient world gazetteer; our aim is to record archaeological locations and places mentioned and provide Atom feeds to highlight the interoperability between the large database and the individual resources that feed into it. This process will both enrich the gazeteer, and increase the discoverability of our publications from specific localities.
